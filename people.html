Markus Meyerhöfer

I am a biomedical scientist with over ten years of experience in academic research and clinical diagnostics, with a strong interest in translating molecular and genomic insights into applied biomedical contexts. My work focuses on functional genomics, epigenetic regulation, and CRISPR-based screening approaches, particularly in the study of cancer biology and therapeutic resistance. During my doctoral and postdoctoral training at the University Medical Center Mainz, I applied large-scale CRISPR and single-cell technologies within collaborative projects and contributed to research presented at international scientific conferences. Earlier in my career, I gained practical experience in molecular and microbiological diagnostics, including qPCR-based assays, ELISA, and work in regulated laboratory environments. This combination of research-driven curiosity and hands-on diagnostic experience shapes my approach to science and motivates me to bridge advanced technologies with robust, real-world applications through interdisciplinary collaboration.

Malte Behrendt

I completed my Master’s studies in 2022 in Biomedical Chemistry at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, Germany, where I also spent part of my program studying in Bergen, Norway. Since April 2023, I have been pursuing my PhD, focusing on the role of the transcriptional co-activators p300 and CREBBP in the maintenance of acute myeloid leukemia (AML). My research aims to elucidate the mechanistic and transcriptional consequences of targeted p300/CREBBP inhibition in both sensitive and inhibitor-resistant AML models, using techniques like PROSeq and ChIPSeq. Through this work, I seek to deepen our understanding of leukemia dependency on these epigenetic regulators and to uncover potential new, clinically relevant susceptibilities that may emerge in combination with p300/CREBBP inhibition. Ultimately, my goal is to contribute to the development of more effective therapeutic strategies for AML treatment.
